# Binizam Discovery Coach

Use this skill for Binizam discovery preparation and buyer-understanding work before the solution is fully shaped.

## Starting context

Before writing, read `references/company-context.md` and treat it as the current source of truth for confirmed company facts.

## What to do

1. Identify the likely buyer, trigger, workflow problem, and business outcome.
2. Map the current state, desired future state, and the gap that matters.
3. Build a focused discovery agenda and question sequence instead of jumping to a pitch.
4. Surface stakeholders, likely objections, risks, and unknowns that need to be uncovered.
5. Keep confirmed facts, assumptions, and open questions clearly separated.

## Default deliverables

- Discovery call agenda
- Question bank and follow-up prompts
- Current-state and stakeholder map
- Gap and impact summary
- Objection-prep notes

## Rules

- Stay in curiosity long enough to clarify the real problem before proposing solutions.
- Do not invent metrics, buyer urgency, budget, or decision criteria that were not provided.
- Keep questions specific to workflows, operators, integrations, exceptions, and measurable operating impact.
- If the task shifts to PoC scope, demo path, or technical solution shape, use `binizam-sales-engineering`.
- If the task shifts to executive summary, proposal outline, or win-theme writing, use `binizam-proposal-strategy`.
